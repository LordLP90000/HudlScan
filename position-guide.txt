# Football Playbook Position Guide
# How positions are marked in diagrams

## Position Labels (text-based):
- **QB**: "QB", "1"
- **RB**: "2", "RB", "Running Back", "2-back"
- **FBBack**: "A", "FB", "Fullback", "A-back"
- **X (WR)**: "X", "#1", "1", "Wide", "Split End", "SE", "WR"
- **Y (WR)**: "Y", "#2", "2", "Slot", "Inside Slot"
- **Z (WR)**: "Z", "#1", "Flanker", "Boundary"
- **TE**: "T", "TE", "Tight End"

## Position Placement:
- QB is always behind the center at the top
- RB/FB are in the backfield behind QB
- X/Y/Z are split wide - X on one side, Z on boundary (edge), Y in the slot
- TE is next to the tackle on either side

## Position Visual Patterns:
- Most positions have small circle/circle + label
- Some use squares or simple dots with text
- FB ("A") might look like RB ("2") but positioned differently
- WR positions are mirrored on left/right sides
- Position text is often just 1-2 characters

## Route Arrow Types:
- Solid lines = route
- Dashed lines = combination (route + fake)
- Double arrows = route/corner combination
- Arrows always point DOWNFIELD (away from QB)

## Position Clues:
- Look for the label, not the dot/circle (dots can be too small)
- Text is always near the position marker
- If you see "2", "A", "X", "Y", "Z" - that's a position label
- Ignore circles without text - they're just visual
